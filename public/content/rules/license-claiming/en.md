# Licensing & Ownership

**Core Rules**:
To maintain a healthy open-source ecosystem, all members must comply with the following:
- Do not sell OEM applications, system apps, or any components that you do not own or have no legal right to distribute.
- Do not steal, rebrand, or claim ownership of any application, source code, device tree (DT), module, script, or other works if you did not contribute to their creation.
- Claiming someone else’s work as your own, either partially or fully, is strictly prohibited.

**Exceptions**:
The above rules do not apply if the work is released under a permissive open-source license that allows commercial use (provided you comply with all license terms), such as:
- MIT License
- BSD License (2-Clause / 3-Clause)
- Apache License 2.0
- ISC License

**Important Notes**:
- “Open source” does not mean free to claim or resell without rules.
- Lack of knowledge about licensing is not an excuse for violations.

**Consequences**: Warnings, sanctions, or FBAN depending on severity.

**Explanation**: Respect the work. Respect the license. Respect the TCF ecosystem.
